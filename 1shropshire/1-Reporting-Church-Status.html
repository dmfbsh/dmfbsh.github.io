Visiting Shropshire's Churches

The plan is to visit all (Church of England) churches in Shropshire that were built prior to the end of the Victorian era.

A total of 317 sites to visit have been identified.

Background

In 1912 the Rev D H S Cranage published the book: An Architectural Account of the Churches of Shropshire, in this book 311 churches in Shropshire are described.

In 2004 (and 2013 with colour photos) John Leonard published the book: The Churches of Shropshire & their treasures, in this later book 322 strictly Anglican churches in Shropshire are described. Of these churches, 11 have been excluded as they either are now private dwellings, or were built post the Victorian era.

In addition, 4 Abbeys (Shrewsbury, Haughmond, Buildwas and Lilleshall) and 2 Priorys (Wenlock and White Ladies) have been added to the list.

Map

The key to the symbols used on the map is:

  • Grey Tick - Church has been visited (outside and inside)
  • Red Tick - Church has been visited (outside only and e.g. found to be locked)
  • Yellow Tick - Planned to be visited and as a priority
  • Yellow Placemark - Planned to be visited
  • Blue Placemark - Yet to be visited

How to use the map:

  • Click on the icon in the top left corner to select which churches to show on the map.
  • Click on the icon in the top right corner to open a larger view of the map.
